What is Hand-Arm Vibration Syndrome ?

Hand-Arm Vibration Syndrome (HAVS) is a serious condition caused by prolonged exposure to vibration from power tools and machinery.

It can lead to permanent damage to blood vessels, nerves, and joints, resulting in symptoms such as:

  • Numbness
  • Tingling
  • Loss of grip strength

To protect workers the HSE (Health and Safety Executive) enforces strict work regulations under the Control of Vibration at work Regulations 2005.

These regulations define vibration exposure levels and specify when immediate action must be taken to reduce risks.

what are the havs exposure limits ?

Employers must monitor workers' daily vibration exposure and ensure that they stay with the legal limits:

  • Daily Exposure Action Value (EAV) - 2.5 m/s² A(8) - At this level, employers must introduce measures to reduce exposure.

  • Daily Exposure Limit Value (ELV) - 5 m/s² A(8) - This is the maximum allowable exposure in a single workday.

how to calculate hand-arm vibration exposure

To calculate hand-arm vibration exposure, multiply the tool's vibration magnitude (m/s²) by the duration of use.

Use a HSE HAVS calculator to determine daily exposure levels, compare them to the EAV (2.5 m/s² A(8)), and take immediate action if limits are exceeded.

hand-arm vibration exposure reference

ℹ < 2.0 m/s² – Low exposure

ℹ 2.0 – <2.5 m/s² – Approaching EAV

⚠ ≥ 2.5 m/s² – Exposure Action Value (EAV)

⚠ 4.0 – 5.0 m/s² – High risk

⛔ > 5.0 m/s² – Exposure Limit Value (ELV)

Exposure points:
100 points = EAV · 400 points = ELV

A(8) represents the daily 8-hour time-weighted vibration exposure.
